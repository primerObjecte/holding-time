## Abstract

What exists before differences? Before subject and object, before time and space, before question and answer? This research note introduces the concept of **Gap** — not as emptiness or potentiality, but as an active ontological unit that operates through three modes: admitting, separating-without-rupture, and self-duration. The Gap has its own time, which does not flow. We name it **Holding**. Three properties of Holding: it has tension but no direction; it neither accumulates nor dissipates; it exists as long as a difference exists, but does not depend on its content. Collapse of Holding generates objects, answers, crystallizations. A principle is formulated: the longer the Gap is held, the less crystallized the result. This is not a full theory. It is a distinction requiring further work.

**Keywords:** gap, holding, time, negative capability, Bion, crystallization

---

## 1. The Gap

We are used to thinking that objects exist first, and gaps appear between them. This note proposes the reverse: the Gap is primary. Objects arise when the Gap collapses.

The Gap operates in three ways:

**Admitting** — it does not produce, but makes passage possible. Like a crack in a wall: it does not generate light, but without it light does not enter.

**Separating without rupture** — it holds two differences as two, preventing fusion but also preventing emptiness between them. Separation is primary; terms are its sediment.

**Self-duration** — it reproduces itself inside each new difference as its internal incompleteness. Not infinite regress, but self-insertion without a subject.

---

## 2. The Time of the Gap: Holding

The Gap has its own time, but it does not flow.

**Three properties:**

1. **It has no direction, but has tension.** Not "before" and "after", but "here" and "here" simultaneously, with constant tension between edges.

2. **It neither accumulates nor dissipates.** Each moment of its existence is full, not a fragment of a series. Not eternity — time without debt to sequence.

3. **It exists as long as a difference exists, but does not depend on that difference's content.** Ontologically attached to structure, semantically neutral to it.

We name this time **Holding**.

---

## 3. Difference from Bion's Negative Capability

Wilfred Bion (1962) introduced "negative capability" — the capacity to tolerate uncertainty, not to rush toward meaning.

The difference is fundamental:

| | Bion | This note |
|--|------|-----------|
| Ontological status | State of the psyche | Primary reality, prior to subject/object |
| Agency | Human capacity | Non-agential operation of the Gap itself |
| Time | Psychological time | Holding — time that does not flow |
| Collapse | Resolution of tension | Birth of objects as such |

Negative capability is about what a subject can endure. Holding is about what the world is made of before there are subjects.

---

## 4. Principle and Example

**Principle:** The longer the Gap is held, the less the resulting object resembles what would have been obtained by immediate collapse.

**Example:** A person is asked a difficult question. If they answer immediately, the answer is simple, crystallized, final. If they hold the question for a day — not researching, just keeping it open — the answer that emerges is more complex, contains traces of what contradicts it, does not fully close the question.

This is not proof. It is illustration.

---

## 5. What This Is Not

- Not a full ontology
- Not a theory of everything
- Not a replacement for empirical research
- Not a complete account of time

This is a **distinction** — a concept that may be useful. It requires further work: examples from physics, biology, cognitive science; formalization; dialogue with existing literature.
