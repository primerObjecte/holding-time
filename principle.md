# Principle and Example

## Principle

> The longer the Gap is held, the less the resulting object resembles what would have been obtained by immediate collapse.

## Example

A person is asked a difficult question.

- **Immediate answer:** simple, crystallized, final. Closes the question.
- **Answer after holding the question for a day:** more complex, contains traces of what contradicts it, does not fully close the question.

## Why this matters

If the principle holds, it suggests that:
- Time pressure forces crystallization
- Holding requires no research — only resistance to closure
- Quality of answers depends not on information, but on duration of holding

## Limitations

This is an illustration, not a proof. Empirical testing would require:
- Controlled comparisons
- Operational definitions of "holding" and "crystallization"
- Domain-specific examples (physics, biology, cognitive science)
